Tokyo tower Akihabara: Famous tourist attraction, and a shopping district, mainly for electronics. Many arcades and anime stores are also located in Akihabara.

Imperial Palace: Once home to the shogun(Emperor of Japan).

Don Quijote: Sells antiques, luxury items, groceries, and a bunch of weird things. Sometimes, they even have some 2nd hand items on sale. It’s a shop worth checking out if pass by, or looking for souvenirs.

Tokyo DisneySea: Tokyo’s version of Disneyland which is supposed to be more appealing to adults.

Chidorigafuchi Park: Located around the Imperial Palace, the place is well known for its beautiful sakura. You can rent a boat and see the scenery around the palace. Keep in mind that the park is also a cemetery for the veterans of WWII.

West Shinjuku: It contains many of Tokyo’s main skyscrapers and also the “Tokyo City Hall”(799 feet). Also, many of department stores like Odakyu Department Store and Keio Department Store are located in West Shinjuku.
